Computer Vision and Obstacle Avoidance

Over the Summer I worked under Prof. Vikram Kapila in the NYU Tandon School of Engineering Mechatronics and Controls Lab. I completed several projects throughout my time there and integrated them before I left: First, I wrote a Python script to track objects and their distances in real time. Second, I used SolidWorks to model a complex 12-piece robotic arm for motion simulation and inverse kinematics. I then wrote a Python class for controlling individual motors. Before I finished my time there, I was able to integrate these all into a Python app that uses object detection and motion tracking to detect objects and ensure that the robotic arm avoids them.

SkyPals

My dad is retired now and spends a lot of time playing the video game Skyrim. I noticed that he spent a lot of time in the game with companions who were not well suited to his play style. There are hundreds of followers in the game, and it can be daunting to find the best one. With that in mind, I created SkyPals. It's a web app that surveys the user for their playstyle and tells them which follower they should use. I wrote it using the Django framework for Python and postgreSQL.
